Sheaves on a basis: the extend-off-basis bridge

Win: build a sheaf on $\operatorname{Spec} R$ by defining it only on the $D(f)$'s Time: ~15 min Needs: Lessons 0002–0003 Ref: Sheaves cheat-sheet

Here is the practical problem. To define $\mathcal O_{\operatorname{Spec} R}$ we know what we want on standard opens — $\mathcal O(D(f))=R_f$ — but specifying sections on every weird open set directly would be a nightmare. The fix is a theorem with real teeth: a sheaf is determined by its values on a basis. Define it on the $D(f)$'s, check the axiom on those, and a unique genuine sheaf on all of $\operatorname{Spec} R$ comes for free.1

1. Stacks Project, Bases and sheaves, Tag 009H (section).

1 · A presheaf on a basis carries less data

Recall a basis $\mathcal B$ of opens: every open is a union of basis elements. For $\operatorname{Spec} R$ the standard opens $D(f)$ are a basis, and they are closed under intersection since $D(f)\cap D(g)=D(fg)$ (Lesson 0001) — a major convenience.

Definition — (pre)sheaf on a basis A presheaf on $\mathcal B$ assigns sections and restriction maps only to basis elements (with identity and transitivity).2 It is a sheaf on $\mathcal B$ if it satisfies the gluing axiom for covers of basis elements by basis elements — re-covering each overlap $U_i\cap U_j$ by basis pieces.3
2. Tag 009I. Warning: a presheaf on $\mathcal B$ holds genuinely less information than a presheaf on all opens — the two notions differ.
3. The sheaf-on-a-basis condition: Tag 009J.
You only ever check finite covers It suffices to verify the axiom on a cofinal system of covers.4 Each $D(f)\cong\operatorname{Spec}(R_f)$ is quasi-compact (Lesson 0001), so finite standard covers $D(f)=\bigcup_{i=1}^{n} D(g_i)$ are cofinal. You never have to wrestle an infinite cover.
4. Cofinal systems of coverings: Tag 009K.

2 · The bridge theorem: extend off the basis

Theorem — extend off the basis A sheaf $\mathcal F$ on a basis $\mathcal B$ extends to a unique sheaf $\mathcal F^{\mathrm{ext}}$ on $X$ with $\mathcal F^{\mathrm{ext}}(U)=\mathcal F(U)$ for $U\in\mathcal B$, compatibly with restriction. In fact restriction-to-$\mathcal B$ is an equivalence of categories $\operatorname{Sh}(X)\xrightarrow{\sim}\operatorname{Sh}(\mathcal B)$, and stalks are unchanged: $\mathcal F^{\mathrm{ext}}_x=\mathcal F_x$.5
5. Extend-off-basis: Tag 009N; the equivalence $\operatorname{Sh}(X)\simeq\operatorname{Sh}(\mathcal B)$: Tag 009O.

Mechanically, $\mathcal F^{\mathrm{ext}}(U)$ is reconstructed as the sections that are locally given by basis data — compatible germs from $\mathcal B$ — exactly the construction that uniqueness forces. The content of the theorem is that this works and is unique.

Everything now snaps together. To build $\mathcal O_{\operatorname{Spec} R}$:

Presheaves & the sheaf condition

Win: decide whether a rule "is a sheaf" by checking gluing on a cover Time: ~15 min Needs: Lesson 0001 ($D(f)$, opens) Ref: Sheaves cheat-sheet

Last lesson you turned a ring into a space. A space alone is too coarse to do geometry on (recall: it isn't even Hausdorff). The missing data — "what are the functions on each open set?" — is packaged by a sheaf. This lesson installs the one axiom that separates a sheaf from a mere bookkeeping table: gluing. It is the exact axiom the structure sheaf $\mathcal O_{\operatorname{Spec} R}$ will have to satisfy.1

1. Stacks Project, ch. Sheaves on Spaces, Tag 006A.

1 · Presheaf = data + restriction

Definition — presheaf A presheaf $\mathcal F$ on a space $X$ assigns to every open $U\subseteq X$ a set $\mathcal F(U)$ (its sections), and to every inclusion $V\subseteq U$ a restriction map $\rho^U_V\colon \mathcal F(U)\to\mathcal F(V)$, such that $\rho^U_U=\mathrm{id}$ and $\rho^V_W\circ\rho^U_V=\rho^U_W$ for $W\subseteq V\subseteq U$. Write $s|_V:=\rho^U_V(s)$.2
2. Tag 006E. Equivalently: a contravariant functor from open sets (and inclusions) to $\mathbf{Set}$ — or to $\mathbf{Ab}$, $\mathbf{Ring}$ if sections carry that structure.
Keep this example forever $\mathcal F(U)=\{\text{functions } U\to k\}$ with honest restriction of functions. Every abstract axiom below should be checked against this picture. The structure sheaf is just this idea with "function" read algebraically — $\mathcal F(D(f))=R_f$.

2 · The sheaf condition: glue + locality

A presheaf lets you restrict. A sheaf also lets you build global from local.

Definition — sheaf A presheaf $\mathcal F$ is a sheaf if for every open cover $U=\bigcup_i U_i$ and every family of sections $s_i\in\mathcal F(U_i)$ that agree on overlaps, $$s_i|_{U_i\cap U_j}=s_j|_{U_i\cap U_j}\quad\text{for all }i,j,$$ there is a unique $s\in\mathcal F(U)$ with $s|_{U_i}=s_i$ for all $i$.3
3. Tag 006T. The condition splits into two clauses, both required.

Read the axiom as two independent demands:

ClauseWhat it guaranteesName
at most one gluea section is determined by its restrictions to any coverseparatedness / locality4
at least one gluecompatible local data actually patch togethergluing / existence
4. Separatedness as injectivity into the product of stalks: Tag 007A (stalks: next lesson).
The forced empty-set convention Apply the axiom to the empty cover of $U=\varnothing$: it forces $\mathcal F(\varnothing)$ to be a single point (the terminal object). And for a disjoint union $U\sqcup V$ it forces $\mathcal F(U\sqcup V)=\mathcal F(U)\times\mathcal F(V)$.5 This is exactly why the constant presheaf $U\mapsto A$ with $|A|\ge 2$ is not a sheaf: it sends $\varnothing$ to $A$, and two different constants on a disconnected open can't be glued.
5. Tag 006U (the "warning" remark). The fix — sheafifying $A$ to locally constant functions — is the constant sheaf $\underline A$, Tag 006W.

3 · Sheaf or not? The reflex to build

Gluing is a real condition, not a formality. The instructive failure to memorize:

Presheaf on $X$Sheaf?Why
continuous functions $U\to\mathbb R$✓ yescontinuity is local; compatible pieces glue uniquely
holomorphic / smooth functions, $k$-forms✓ yesall checked locally / in charts
sections of a map $p\colon E\to X$✓ yesthe prototype — "sheaf of sections"
bounded continuous functions✗ noidentity holds, but pieces bounded on $(n{-}1,n{+}1)$ glue to $f(x)=x$ — unbounded; gluing fails
functions that extend to all of $X$✗ no"extendable" isn't local: $1/x$ is locally a restriction near each point of $\mathbb R\setminus\{0\}$ but doesn't extend; gluing fails
constant presheaf $U\mapsto A$, $|A|\ge2$✗ nofails at $\varnothing$ and on disconnected opens; both clauses
The bridge to the structure sheaf When you build $\mathcal O_{\operatorname{Spec} R}$, the cover will be a standard cover $D(f)=\bigcup_i D(g_i)$, the overlaps will be $D(g_i)\cap D(g_j)=D(g_ig_j)$ (from Lesson 0001!), and "agree on overlaps + glue uniquely" will become a single exact sequence of localizations $$0\to R_f\to\textstyle\prod_i R_{g_i}\rightrightarrows\prod_{i,j} R_{g_ig_j}.$$ Drilling existence-and-uniqueness now turns that future step into recognition, not new learning.

Play: the gluing lab

Pick a presheaf, then press Attempt to glue the compatible local data over its cover. Watch which ones patch into a global section and which break — and on exactly which clause.

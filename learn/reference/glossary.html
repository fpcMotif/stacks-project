Glossary & the Algebra↔Geometry Dictionary

The canonical vocabulary for this course. Terms here are used consistently across all lessons. Every entry links to the Stacks Project tag that pins it down precisely. Built to print on one or two pages for quick reference.

Core terms

Spectrum  $\operatorname{Spec}(R)$ The set of all prime ideals of a (commutative, unital) ring $R$, equipped with the Zariski topology. The fundamental object: it turns a ring into a space. A point of $\operatorname{Spec}(R)$ is a prime ideal $\mathfrak p$. Tag 00DZ
$V(I)$ — vanishing / closed set For an ideal (or subset) $I\subseteq R$, $V(I)=\{\mathfrak p\in\operatorname{Spec}(R)\mid I\subseteq\mathfrak p\}$. These are exactly the closed sets of the Zariski topology. Think "points where every function in $I$ vanishes." Tag 00E0
$D(f)$ — distinguished / standard open $D(f)=\{\mathfrak p\mid f\notin\mathfrak p\}=\operatorname{Spec}(R)\setminus V(f)$: the locus where $f$ is "nonzero." The $D(f)$ form a basis for the topology, and $D(f)\cong\operatorname{Spec}(R_f)$. Tag 00E4
Zariski topology The topology on $\operatorname{Spec}(R)$ whose closed sets are the $V(I)$. It is generally not Hausdorff; it is always quasi-compact. Tag 00E0
Generic point A point whose closure is a whole irreducible closed set. For an integral domain, the prime $(0)$ is a generic point dense in all of $\operatorname{Spec}(R)$. Irreducible closed subsets $\leftrightarrow$ their unique generic points. Tag 00ES
Closed point A point $\{\mathfrak p\}$ that is closed, i.e. $\mathfrak p$ is a maximal ideal. In $\operatorname{Spec}(R)$, $\overline{\{\mathfrak p\}}=V(\mathfrak p)$. Tag 00E5
Quasi-compact Every open cover has a finite subcover (the term "compact" is reserved for quasi-compact + Hausdorff in this field). $\operatorname{Spec}(R)$ is always quasi-compact. Tag 00E8
Structure sheaf $\mathcal O_X$  (preview, lesson 0002) The sheaf of rings on $X=\operatorname{Spec}(R)$ with $\mathcal O_X(D(f))=R_f$; its stalk at $\mathfrak p$ is the local ring $R_{\mathfrak p}$. Makes $X$ a locally ringed space. Tag 01HR
Affine scheme  (preview, lesson 0002) A locally ringed space isomorphic to $(\operatorname{Spec}(R),\mathcal O_{\operatorname{Spec}(R)})$. A scheme is a space locally isomorphic to affine schemes. Tag 01HW

Sheaf terms

Presheaf An assignment $U\mapsto\mathcal F(U)$ (sections) on opens, with restriction maps $\rho^U_V\colon\mathcal F(U)\to\mathcal F(V)$ for $V\subseteq U$ satisfying $\rho^U_U=\mathrm{id}$ and $\rho^V_W\rho^U_V=\rho^U_W$ — a contravariant functor on opens. $s|_V:=\rho^U_V(s)$. Tag 006E
Sheaf A presheaf in which compatible local sections glue uniquely: for any cover $U=\bigcup U_i$ and $s_i$ agreeing on overlaps, a unique $s\in\mathcal F(U)$ restricts to each $s_i$. Two clauses: gluing (existence) and separatedness/locality (uniqueness). Tag 006T
Separated presheaf The uniqueness half alone: $\mathcal F(U)\to\prod_{x\in U}\mathcal F_x$ is injective — a section is determined by its germs. Tag 007A
Stalk $\mathcal F_x$ & germ $s_x$ $\mathcal F_x=\operatorname*{colim}_{x\in U}\mathcal F(U)$, the filtered colimit over neighborhoods of $x$ (reverse inclusion). A germ $s_x$ is a section seen arbitrarily near $x$: $(U,s)\sim(U',s')$ iff they agree on some smaller $U''\ni x$. For the structure sheaf, $\mathcal O_{X,\mathfrak p}=R_{\mathfrak p}$. Tag 0078
Sheafification $\mathcal F^{\#}$ The best sheaf approximating a presheaf $\mathcal F$ (left adjoint to the inclusion of sheaves), built from compatible germs; $\mathcal F^{\#}_x=\mathcal F_x$. E.g. the constant sheaf $\underline A$ is the sheafification of the constant presheaf. Tag 007X
(Pre)sheaf on a basis A (pre)sheaf defined only on a basis $\mathcal B$ of opens (e.g. the $D(f)$). A sheaf on $\mathcal B$ extends to a unique sheaf on $X$, with $\operatorname{Sh}(X)\simeq\operatorname{Sh}(\mathcal B)$ and unchanged stalks — the mechanism that builds $\mathcal O_{\operatorname{Spec} R}$. Tag 009H, extend-off-basis Tag 009N
Locally ringed space A space with a sheaf of rings $\mathcal O_X$ all of whose stalks are local rings. The "locally" is exactly the stalks $R_{\mathfrak p}$ being local. Affine schemes, and hence schemes, are locally ringed spaces. Tag 01HB

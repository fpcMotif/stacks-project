# Mission: Understand Schemes (Modern Algebraic Geometry)

## Why
You have a solid grip on commutative algebra (rings, modules, localization,
the Nullstellensatz) and want to cross the bridge into **scheme theory** —
to genuinely *understand* schemes, not just recognize the definitions. The
goal is deep, durable mastery for its own sake: to be able to read the
Stacks Project and modern algebraic geometry fluently and reason about
geometric objects through their rings.

## Success looks like
- Look at a ring and **see a space**: describe `Spec(R)` — its points, its
  topology, its structure sheaf — and compute it for concrete rings.
- Read a scheme-theoretic statement (separated, proper, flat, smooth, étale
  morphisms) and unpack what it *means* geometrically and algebraically.
- Translate freely in both directions across the **algebra ↔ geometry
  dictionary** (ideals ↔ closed sets, localization ↔ restriction, etc.).
- Navigate the Stacks Project by tag and follow a proof end-to-end.

## Constraints
- Self-paced, no deadline. Optimize for **storage strength** (long-term
  retention) over fluency, using retrieval practice and spacing.
- Strong commutative-algebra background — lessons may *use* CA freely, and
  should explicitly anchor new scheme ideas to the algebra already known.
- Workspace lives in `./learn/` inside the live Stacks Project repo, so it
  never pollutes the repo's git status or upstream syncs.

## Out of scope (for now)
- Algebraic spaces & stacks (the namesake material) — revisit once ordinary
  schemes are solid.
- Étale / crystalline cohomology and the deep cohomology machinery.
- Classical varieties via the Italian school / purely Hartshorne Ch. I lens —
  we go scheme-first.

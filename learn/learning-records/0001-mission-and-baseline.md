# Mission set: deep self-study of schemes, from a commutative-algebra baseline

Established at workspace creation (no prior records). The learner wants
durable mastery of **scheme theory** for its own sake, starting from a solid
commutative-algebra background (rings, modules, localization, Nullstellensatz)
but treating schemes as new/shaky. See [[MISSION.md]].

## Implications
- Teach scheme-first, anchoring each new idea to known commutative algebra.
- Lesson 0001 starts at the algebra→geometry bridge: `Spec(R)` as a
  topological space. CA prerequisites (primes, radicals, localization) are
  assumed known, not re-taught — they are the *handle* on the new material.
- Out of scope for now: algebraic spaces/stacks, deep cohomology. Optimize
  for storage strength (spacing + retrieval), not speed.

## Baseline assumed (to verify as we go)
- Comfortable: prime/maximal ideals, radicals, localization, quotient rings.
- To confirm before lesson 0002: comfort with sheaves (needed for the
  structure sheaf and locally ringed spaces).
